How EAM Software Works

Procurement Governance

Purchase Requisition, Purchase Order, and Goods Receipt Note — each with configurable multi-level approval matrices. Every procurement decision is recorded with frozen approval snapshots, so the audit trail shows exactly who approved what and when.

Asset Register

Fixed Assets, Inventory, Consumables, Low-Value Assets, and Scrap — all in one register with 50+ fields per record. Assets are auto-created on goods receipt posting. Parent-child relationships, location-based ID sequencing, and full ownership history are maintained from day one.

Depreciation Engine

Straight-Line Method (SLM) and Written-Down Value (WDV), configurable per depreciation class. Class-based rates aligned with Schedule II of the Companies Act. Runtime calculation for current values, year-end snapshots for financial statements, and a Fixed Asset Schedule (FAS) report.

Physical Verification

QR tag generation with globally unique sequencing. Print labels, apply to assets, and scan with any phone browser — no app install required. Verification campaigns track which assets are verified, missing, or in poor condition. Discrepancy reports are ready for auditors.

Vendor & Contract Management

Full vendor lifecycle — registration, document verification, compliance tracking. AMC and warranty contract management with renewal alerts. Vendor debit notes for invoice mismatches and quality issues. All vendor interactions linked to procurement records.

Accounting Integration

Tally-compatible XML voucher generation covering 22 event types — purchase entries, capitalisation, depreciation, movement transfers, write-offs, scrap disposal, and vendor debit notes. Direct import into Tally without manual journal entries. Reconciliation between asset register and general ledger.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is EAM software?

Enterprise Asset Management (EAM) software is a system that manages the full lifecycle of physical assets — from procurement and receipt through registration, depreciation, maintenance, verification, and eventual disposal. It maintains a centralised register, tracks ownership and location, calculates depreciation, and generates the accounting entries needed for financial reporting.

How is EAM different from ERP?

An ERP system covers broad business functions — HR, payroll, sales, inventory, finance — with asset management as one small module. EAM software focuses entirely on asset lifecycle management with deeper capabilities: multi-level procurement approval, class-based depreciation, QR tagging, physical verification campaigns, and detailed audit trails. Organisations that need governance depth over breadth often find EAM more practical than an ERP asset module.

What asset types does the register support?

The register supports five asset classifications in a single system: Fixed Assets (capitalised, depreciated), Inventory (tracked stock items), Consumables (expensed on receipt), Low-Value Assets (below capitalisation threshold), and Scrap (write-off and disposal tracking). Each classification follows its own accounting treatment while sharing the same register and audit trail.

Can EAM software integrate with Tally?

Yes. ProcureTrail generates Tally-compatible XML vouchers for 22 event types across the asset lifecycle — purchase entries, capitalisation, depreciation, movement transfers, write-offs, scrap disposal, and vendor debit notes. The XML output imports directly into Tally without manual journal entries, keeping the asset register and accounting system synchronised.

Is EAM suitable for small and mid-size organisations?

Yes. Unlike traditional EAM platforms that require large implementation teams and per-user licensing, ProcureTrail is delivered as a professional service with no per-seat cost. Organisations with 50 to 5,000 assets benefit from the same governance and audit trail as larger enterprises — without the infrastructure overhead of a full ERP deployment.
